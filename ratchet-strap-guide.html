How to Use Ratchet Straps Safely 2026

The right way to secure cargo with ratchet straps — avoid the mistakes that cause load shifts and accidents.

Why Cargo Securing Matters

An unsecured load can shift at highway speeds, damage your vehicle, and create serious road hazards. DOT regulations require cargo to be properly secured — and for good reason.

Choosing the Right Strap

Match the strap's Working Load Limit (WLL) to your cargo weight. A good rule: use straps with a combined WLL at least 1.5x the weight of your load. For heavy equipment, go 2x.

Step 1: Inspect Before Use

Check for fraying, cuts, or damaged hooks before every use. A compromised strap can fail under load. Replace any strap that shows wear — they're cheap compared to the alternative.

Step 2: Anchor Points

Use rated anchor points — D-rings, E-track, or factory tie-down loops. Never anchor to bumpers, tailgates, or non-rated points. Check your vehicle's owner manual for rated anchor locations.

Step 3: Route the Strap

Run straps over and around the load, not just across the top. Use at least 4 straps for large loads — front, rear, and both sides. Avoid sharp edges that can cut the webbing.

Step 4: Ratchet to Tension

Ratchet until the strap is taut with no slack. Don't over-tighten — you can damage soft cargo or deform the load. The strap should be firm, not guitar-string tight.

Step 5: Check at Every Stop

Straps can loosen as cargo settles. Check tension after the first 10 miles and at every fuel stop on long hauls.
